Chandrayaan

Chandrayaan, India's lunar exploration mission, symbolizes the nation's technological prowess. Launched by ISRO, it aims to explore the Moon's surface and geology. With advanced instruments onboard, including a lunar orbiter, lander, and rover, Chandrayaan seeks to uncover mysteries about the Moon's composition and resources.

Project.
Bhoonidhi

Bhoonidhi" grants access to an extensive archive of remote sensing data from 46 satellites, both Indian and foreign, spanning over 33 years. This invaluable resource empowers users with comprehensive insights into Earth's dynamics and environmental changes. Utilizing advanced technology, Bhoonidhi facilitates seamless retrieval and analysis of satellite imagery for various applications. Join us as Bhoonidhi revolutionizes Earth observation and drives sustainable development initiatives worldwide.

Misson.
Aditya-L1

Aditya-L1, India's solar exploration mission, marks a significant leap in space research. Spearheaded by ISRO, it aims to study the Sun's corona and its dynamic processes. Equipped with cutting-edge instruments, Aditya-L1 seeks to unravel mysteries about solar phenomena, aiding in space weather prediction. This mission underscores India's commitment to advancing scientific knowledge in solar physics. Join us as Aditya-L1 embarks on a pioneering journey to explore our nearest star, the Sun.

Misson.
Gaganyaan

"Gaganyaan," an emblem of India's space pioneering, represents ISRO's leap into human spaceflight. With meticulous planning and cutting-edge technology, ISRO aims to send Indian astronauts into space, marking a historic milestone. Gaganyaan embodies India's aspirations to join the esteemed league of nations with crewed space missions, showcasing our scientific prowess and inspiring future generations. Join us as Gaganyaan embarks on a transformative odyssey, unlocking new frontiers in space exploration.
